B · Workflow Fit
⚠AES passport-only verification: SignNow's standard AES uses email-based identity. For stricter AES compliance (eIDAS Level 2), a qualified trust service provider (QTSP) certificate is needed. If AISB's legal team expects QTSP-level AES, standard signNow AES may not satisfy — this needs to be scoped before the June bulk send, not after.
⚠No integrations purchased: AISB likely uses a school management system (e.g. Managebac, PowerSchool, or similar). Without integration, completed contracts will not auto-flow back to the student record system — manual download/upload may create admin friction at scale. Flag as a potential future need.
C · Industry & Regulatory
⚠eIDAS AES in Hungary: Hungary has fully transposed eIDAS — AES is legally binding for most institutional contracts including school enrollment agreements. However, Hungary's National Media and Infocommunications Authority (NMHH) and data protection authority (NAIH) both have specific requirements around consent and personal data processing in digital workflows. AISB must ensure their signNow workflow includes proper GDPR consent language in the document templates for EU data subjects.
⚠Non-EU signer jurisdiction: For families who are not EU residents at time of signing, the relevant legal framework shifts to their home country's eSignature law (US ESIGN/UETA, etc.). This is generally less restrictive than eIDAS — US and most international frameworks accept email-authenticated eSignatures as binding. Dex should confirm with AISB that their legal team has reviewed this point, and document it in the kickoff call notes.
⚠eIDAS 2.0 transition (2026): The updated eIDAS regulation introduces the EU Digital Identity Wallet which will change identity verification flows across the EU. No immediate action required, but AISB's IT/legal team may raise questions — position this as a future improvement, not a current gap.
